In 1978, Haruki Murakami was 29 and running a jazz bar in downtown Tokyo. One April day, The impulse to write a novel came to him suddenly while watching game. That first novel, Hear the Wing Sing, won a new writers' award and was published the following year. More followed, but It was Norwegian Wood, published in 1987, which turned Murakami from a writer to a phenomenon
